Visualising Global Smoking Trends (1980 to 2012)

32202385, Data Visualisation 2

The act of smoking cigarettes is harmful, causing many kinds of detrimental effects to the health of smokers. Despite this, a lot of people worldwide light up and smoke several cigarettes daily.

What does the smoking habit look like for all the countries in the whole world, over time?

Global Metrics

Interact with the region selector to view the corresponding top 10 metrics for a specific region, and the year slider to view the three diagrams below corresponding to a year from 1980 to 2012.

Observations Include:

  • Globally overall, the portion of smoking populations seem to have dropped over the years.
  • A high amount of countries with the top 10 highest smoking prevalences yearly are located in Europe.
  • Suriname (South America) smokers, smoke the most amount of cigarettes on average since 1989, and they consistently smoke more than 100 cigarettes daily on average from 1980 to 2012!

Region Comparison

The two charts to the left showcase each region's countries' average daily cigarette consumption per smoker.

The line chart of region averages is a total naive average of the metric, by the number of countries in the region.

What does this tell?

  • The worldwide average daily cigarette consumption by a smoker has very slightly dropped over the three decades.
  • All regions of the world have dropped, or maintained their average daily smokers' cigarette intake.
  • The average smoker in Europe and Americas recently would smoke noticeably more cigarettes on average in a day, than the worldwide average.
  • Asia's average cigarette consumption is not too far off of the world average.
  • Oceania's and Africa's average cigarette counts are below the world average, with the latter not exceeding the world average in both mean and median metrics.

Do note that some regions (i.e. The Americas) have countries that are considered outliers, as their citizens consume a very high amount of cigarettes daily on average.
